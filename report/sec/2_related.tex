\textbf{VLMs as evaluators and robot supervisors.} VLMs have been proposed for action-prediction as well as supervision, where supervision can be viewed as a mandatory subtask of the former. As standalone supervision models, they are used as judges for production outcomes, high-level planning, gating logic for the next action phases, and reward models~\cite{brohan2023rt2}\TODO{VoxPoser, success-detector citations}.

But they rarely leverage robotic real-world data and concurrently do not address robot specific difficulties such as complex cross-object dependencies, task-specifics, visually obstructed scenes and temporal reasoning. 

\textbf{Benchmarking VLMs.}
Existing VLM benchmarks focus primarily on general semantic reasoning tasks~\cite{li2025benchmark,yang2025embodiedbench}, while robotic VQA datasets such as Robo2VLM-1~\cite{chen2025robo2vlm} evaluate embodied visual understanding from robot trajectories.

\textbf{VQA from robot trajectories.} Robo2VLM-1~\cite{chen2025robo2vlm} automatically generates visual-question-answering pairs from real robot trajectories and multiple synchronized views (top-left, top-right, wrist) using
DROID~\cite{khazatsky2024droid} and Open X-Embodiment (OXE) data, deriving ground-truth answers from sensor and trajectory metadata rather than human annotation; they cover a wide range of questions from spatial reasoning to task state goal. A majority of these questions rely on visual annotations of the images, such as inserted dots, arrows etc. However they are not designed for failure mode detection specifically and neglect multiview consistency questions. Inadvertiently increasing VQA complexity, the model has to understand the visual modifications in the image and reason correctly internally, adding a layer of abstraction beyond what is necessary in real-world deployment. 

\textbf{Failure modes and Multi view consistency.} Current benchmarks fail to correctly assess the capabilities in these domains. Our work differs in two ways: We evaluate both failure-mode detection and multi-view consistency on the same real-robot data, and we decompose results on zero-shot, CoT-prompting and fine-tuning gains by question type, scene and answer class rather than reporting a single headline accuracy number.

Recent advances in Vision-Language-Models~(VLM) brought LLM-type scaling and reasoning into robotics. While these models perform strongly on object identity and -position queries, state-change, contact and temporal zero-shot and reasoning remain comparatively weak~\cite{lin2025vlmsurvey}. Yet robot supervision depends on understanding these exact dynamics.

% As VLMs are used increasingly used for next-action prediction, task planning and supervision, they require state understanding of the robot and the objects it interacts with, given the context of the task. To bridge the gap to real-world deployment, the models have to be self-sufficient, thus generalizing to unforeseen environments, tasks, and edge cases, relieving the user from finetuning the model and modifying the environment to prevent OOD situations. Thus it is necessary for these models to reliably track progress during a task, identify which sub-task the robot is currently attempting, and understand success/failure of robotic actions across diverse tasks and scenes. We investigate the zero-shot capabilities of different model families and sizes for failure mode detection and multi-view consistency, to determine under which task-specific and scene conditions the models' effectiveness collapses. 

We study two capabilities required for reliable robot supervision. First, failure-mode detection: given multiple views of the scene, can a VLM judge task progress, identify the current action phase, and determine whether action-phase execution succeeded or failed? Low performance implies a lack of semantic scene-comphrension.
Second, multi-view consistency: do answers extracted from different camera angles of the same moment agree with each other? Inconsistency across views would
undermine true scene understanding and entails intensive calibration phase.

To answer these questions, we build a new benchmark on Robo2VLM-1~\cite{chen2025robo2vlm} (DROID~\cite{khazatsky2024droid} and OXE
trajectories), covering 47 scenes and 6224 questions for failure-model detection and 6902 questions for multiview consistency across four question types. We evaluate seven VLMs across three families zero-shot, ablate chain-of-thought~(CoT) prompting, analyze accuracy as a function of task- and scene-difficulty, and run a LoRA fine-tuning case study on Qwen3-4B with a decomposition of the performance gains.
Our contributions are as follows:
\begin{itemize}
  \item New benchmark: 6224 questions / 47 scenes on Robo2VLM-1 (DROID), covering
        failure-mode detection (4 sub-tasks) and multi-view consistency.
  \item Self-designed question types (action phase, phase success, progress, task success,
        multi-view consistency), to test temporal- and state-reasoning. 
  \item Zero-shot evaluation of 7 VLMs across 3 families (Gemma3, Qwen3, InternVL3) with easy/hard scene-difficulty breakdown.
  \item LoRA fine-tuning case study (Qwen3-4B) and CoT prompting ablation with detailed gain decomposition.     
  \item Scene- and task-level dissection of task-specific difficulty.
\end{itemize}

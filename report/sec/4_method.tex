\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}
% LENGTH TARGET: ~0.75 page

Because Robo2VLM-1~\cite{chen2025robo2vlm} trajectories contain only successful executions, our benchmark exploits temporal ordering and action-phase-label--vision alignment to test model capabilities across object, state, manipulation, and scene understanding, with particular emphasis on temporal comprehension of robotic tasks.

\subsection{Metrics}
We measure consistency as whether
independently elicited answers agree across views, using three metrics: TL$\leftrightarrow$TR
agreement, agreement of at least one top view with the wrist view
($\geq$1~Top$\leftrightarrow$W), and All-3-agree, where all three views give the same
answer. Treating each view as an independent binary draw gives baselines of 50\%, 75\%,
and 25\% for the three metrics, respectively.

\subsection{Models}
We evaluate seven publicly available VLMs spanning three model families, chosen to cover
a range of parameter counts within and across families: Gemma3~\cite{gemma2025gemma3}
(E2B, E4B, 31B), Qwen3~\cite{qwen2025qwen3} (4B, 8B, 32B), and
InternVL3~\cite{chen2025internvl3} (14B). All models are run zero-shot on the ETH Zurich
student cluster without any task-specific training.

\subsection{Zero-Shot Evaluation Setup}
The models are prompted with a single image (or two, for \texttt{progress}), the goal
text, and a multiple-choice question with answer options labeled A through E (the exact
count depends on the sub-task). We extract the first letter predicted in a single forward
pass as the model's answer, without few-shot examples or task-specific instructions. In
the chain-of-thought (CoT) variant, the model is instead asked to reason freely before
stating a final answer letter; we apply the same extraction procedure to the last letter
emitted in the response. 

\subsection{LoRA Fine-Tuning}
We additionally fine-tune Qwen3-4B with LoRA to test whether low zero-shot accuracy
reflects missing task-specific grounding rather than a fundamental reasoning ceiling.
Full hyperparameters and the train/val/test split are in \cref{sec:suppl-lora}.
